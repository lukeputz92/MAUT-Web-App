Multi-Attribute Utility Theory

Multi-Attribute Utility Theory or MAUT is an objective twist to making subjective decisions. This site will help you make those hard decisions. If you have any suggestions you can contact us either at the bottom of this page or under the 'Menu' drop down.

WHAT DOES IT DO?

MAUT reads in from hosted API's (Application Programming Interface). Then the user takes what is important to them. By applying scores to what is important it will objectively rank what you "should" consider based on what is important to you. Click the button below for step by step instructions.

Step by Step instructions

Welcome! Here I will show what to do to make that confused decision so much more clear. Buckle up and enjoy the step by step ride to making decisions using API's or your own custom decisions.

Custom Decision

Start Here!

First pick what you want to decide on. Example: Books

Item Selection

Now you have to add all the items that are important to you. Example: Harry Potter series, Song of Ice and Fire, or Lord of the Rings

Add Weight

Here You want to add the criteria and weight for which separates the Items from each other. Weight them with higher numbers meaning more important. Example: Page numbers, User Ratings, or Genre

Score those Items!

Score those items you picked with a subjective ranking. These numbers are limited only by a max value of 100. Example: Harry Potter series are long so I would rank it lower, maybe 2 or 3. The genre however is wonderful so I would rank it at about 30 or so.



Results!

Now our software will spit out an objective ranking of your subjective decisions
